The 8-Week Syllabus

This is where technique becomes usable—clear, practical, and built for real performance.

Week 1 — The Actor’s Foundation Stanislavski
  • Focus: Given circumstances, objectives, truth
  • What is happening? What do I want? Why now?
👉 So performances stop feeling vague and start feeling grounded
Week 2 — Living Truthfully Meisner
  • Focus: Listening, reacting, being present
  • Getting out of your head & behavior over performance
👉 So scenes feel alive, not rehearsed
Week 3 — Emotional Access Strasberg
  • Focus: Personalization, emotional connection
  • Safe emotional recall & substitution without forcing
👉 So emotion becomes specific—not generic
Week 4 — Imagination & Circumstance Adler
  • Focus: Building reality through imagination
  • Expanding beyond personal experience to make the world real
👉 So actors aren’t limited by their own life
Week 5 — Physical Expression Chekhov
  • Focus: Body leads emotion
  • Psychological gesture, energy, and movement
👉 So characters become visible, not just internal
Week 6 — Action-Based Acting Mamet / Practical Aesthetics
  • Focus: Playing actions, not emotions
  • What am I doing to the other person? Simplicity and clarity.
👉 So actors stop overthinking and start doing
Week 7 — Integration Lab Blending Methods
  • Focus: When to use what & finding your personal process
  • Applying tools directly to scene work
👉 So actors build their own working system
Week 8 — Performance & Application Reel Capture
  • Focus: Real scene work using chosen tools
  • Rehearse both scenes, refine performance, and record polished self-tapes
👉 So everything translates to actual performance and tangible assets
